No Longer Human

No Longer Human is a novel by Osamu Dazai. It tells the story of Yozo Oba,
a young man who struggles with a sense of alienation and detachment from society.
The book explores themes of identity, existentialism, and the human condition.

Plot Summary

No Longer Human follows the life of Yozo Oba, a troubled individual who wears a mask of cheerfulness to
hide his true self. As he navigates through various relationships and societal expectations,
he grapples with his own sense of identity and the feeling of being
disconnected from the world around him.

Character Analysis

Yozo Oba is a complex character who embodies the struggles of many individuals in society. His constant
search for acceptance and his inability to connect with others make him a relatable and tragic figure.
Through Yozo's experiences, the novel explores the themes of alienation, self-deception, and the search
for authenticity.

Personal Reflection

No Longer Human is a haunting and introspective novel that delves deep into the human psyche. It raises
profound questions about the masks we wear and the true nature of our existence. Reading this book left
me contemplating the complexities of human identity and the societal pressures that can lead to a sense of detachment.
